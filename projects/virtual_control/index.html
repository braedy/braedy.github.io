Augmented Reality control and rendering. The scene is virtually controlled through physical interaction with the real-world.

  • Technologies: Unity, Qualcomm Vuforia SDK, Android SDK Tools
  • Course: Advanced Visual Interfaces / Avancerade visuella gränssnitt
    @ Uppsala University, Sweden
  • Year: Fall 2014

This project is the primary part of practical (or eventually theoretical) work in the course where one could define individually to gain a deeper experience in a specific field of my preference. Projects could be either programming/development work on a very low level or up to system integration or studies of various kinds (experimental studies, review studies, etc.). I chose to research, implement, and review virtual control in augmented reality interfaces.

Work revolved around identifying an delineating a limited problem within AVI, formulating a related research question or hypothesis, planning a study/project (choice of methods and time frame), conducting the study, and reporting own results.

Tasks were performed self-dependently throughout all stages of the project, involving a proposal delivery, a final project oral presentation, quality of response throughout the project work and a written report (bottom of page).

Augmented Reality scene design in Unity: The scene is mapped to be rendered on the image plane.
