Listed here are some of the results I've produced working with OpenGL and GLSL during the course in Computer Graphics at Uppsala.

Problems rendered started from simple Lambertian model diffuse shading, transformations, and alpha blending to Blinn-Phong shading, implementation of skyboxes and shading via environmental mapping.

Combined with a maximum score for the shadow-mapping mini-project, and a 39,5/40 written exam, the work here contributed to the maximum grade for the 10 ECTS credit course.

Rabbit rendered with tweakable Blinn-Phong shading

Computing Blinn-Phong shading in GLSL vertex and fragment shaders with dynamic variables (configurable through an AntTweakBar).

Gargoyle rendered with environmental shading

To render a reflective/refractive surface required compution of the reflection vector in the fragment shader. This was used as a 3D texture co-ordinate to fetch the incoming light (RGB colour) from the skybox cube map.

Armadillo rendered with environmental shading and specular smoothing filter

Standard mapping produces perfectly smooth, mirror-like surfaces. Pre-filtering the cube map with a smoothing filter based on the specular power of the Blinn-Phong shading model simulates roughness or gloss to the surface. Switching between the filters has been implemented via assigned keys.
